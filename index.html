What is Bullying?

Bullying usually includes three key elements: intent, repetition, and power. A bully intends to cause harm—either physical or emotional—and does so repeatedly. Boys often experience more physical bullying, while girls may face psychological forms.

Types of Bullying

There are different types of bullying: cyber, social, physical, verbal, sexual, and emotional bullying.

Physical Bullying

Includes hitting, kicking, shoving, spitting, stealing, or damaging property. It can harm a person’s body and confidence. Prevent it by encouraging open communication, teaching safety, and promoting empathy.

Cyber Bullying

Cyberbullying involves harassment or humiliation using phones, computers, or tablets. It can include spreading false information or sharing personal data to embarrass someone. Prevention starts with privacy settings, strong passwords, and thinking before posting.
